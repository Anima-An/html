BMW E60

BMW e60 is part of the fifth generation of the BMW 5 series executive cars that consist of the BMW E60 (saloon version) and BMW E61 (wagon version, marketed as 'Touring'). This generation was produced by BMW from 2003 till 2010 and is often referred as the E60.

The E60 introduced various new electronic features such as active cruise control, active stearing, adaptive headlights, night vison and lane departure warning. The E60 was also the first 5 series to be available with a turbocharged petrol engine, a 6-speed automatic transmisson and regenerative braking.

The M5 model was introduced in 2005 and is powered by the BMW S85 V10 engine. It was soldin the saloon and wagon body styles, with most using the 7-speed SMG III transmisson. It was the first and only M5 model to be sold with the V10 engine.

Engines

Petrol

At launch, the E60 used the E39 5 Series' M54 straight 6 engine in the 520i, 525i and the 530i models. Thew only petrol models with the new engnine at lauch was the 545i with the N62 V8. The M54 was taken out after the 2005 model years and replaced by the straight 4 N43 (in the 520i) and the straight 6 N52 engine (in the 525i and 530i). The model range was expanded the same year to include a wider range of models like the 523i, 540i and 550i. In 2007, the only turbocharged petrol E60 model was introduced, the 535i with the twin-turbo straight 6 N54 engine though this model was exclusive only to the North American market and was not sold in Europe.

Diesel

At launch, the only diesel model available was the 530d, which used an updated "TU" version of the M57 engine found in the 4th gen E39 5 Series. In 2004, the 535d and the 525d were added to the range which used a different version of the M57 straight 6 turbo engine. In 2005, the only 4 cylinder diesel model E60 was added to the range of medels, thw 520d using the older M47 engine at first, and later, N47 engine from 2007. Msot of the diesel range was exclusive to the EU market, except a few models whcih were sold in some asian countries. However, no diesel models were offered in the NA market.

M5 Model

The M5 was intoduced in 2005 and produced as saloon and wagon body styles only. The M5 is powered by BMW S85 V10 engine, which produced a respectable 373kW (500 hp) at 7,750 rpm, 520 N⋅m (384 lb⋅ft) at 6,100 rpm and has a redline of 8,250 rpm. The 0-100 km/h (0-62 mph) for this car is 4.7 seconds.

The majority of the M5s were sold with the 7 speeed automated manual transmission (SMG III), however a 6 speed manual was also availabe in some markets, specifically prominent in the NA car market. The total producation of the M5 model for its 5 year run was 20,548 units, consisting of 19,523 saloonms and 1,025 tourings.

Special models

Alpina B5

The Alpina B5 and Alpina B5 S were introduced in February 2005. They were available in both sedan and wagon body styles and were equipped with the 4.4 L (269 cu in) BMW N62 petrol V8 engine, which was also used in the 545i. Additionally, these models featured a centrifugal supercharger. The B5 had a rated power output of 368 kW (493 hp), while the more powerful B5 S boasted 390 kW (523 hp). Both models used the ZF 6HP26 6-speed hydraulic automatic transmission, which was shared with the regular E60 models.

BMW 5 Series Security

The E60 lineup included 'Security' models, which were factory-armored versions unveiled at the 2005 Frankfurt Motor Show. These vehicles were constructed with a combination of ballistic steel, aramid, polyethylene, and 21mm polycarbonate layered glass. They achieved certification at the VR4 level, according to VPAM's Bullet Resistant Vehicle guidelines from 1999. The armored areas covered the entire passenger cell, battery terminals, and the Engine Control Unit. Additionally, these models came with standard features such as an intercom system and a panic alarm.

Production

The E60/E61 series was produced at the BMW Group Plant in Dingolfing, Germany, as well as at the BMW Brilliance plant in China. Furthermore, German-manufactured kits for these vehicles were assembled through complete knock-down processes in Thailand, Egypt, Russia, and Malaysia.
